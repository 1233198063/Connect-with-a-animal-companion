The User Problem

All respondents expressed their love for animals. Some, however, are unable to keep pets due to factors such as their living environment, financial situation, or busy work and study schedules. Despite this, they still hope to connect with animals, for example, by volunteering at animal shelters, and they wish for channels that could help them establish that connection. Others are currently keeping or planning to adopt pets and hope to obtain information on pet adoption or purchase.

Therefore, our Pet Planet app will focus on these areas, providing people with information and opportunities to connect with animals.

Understanding user pain points

User Interviews

Through these interviews, I learned that people engage with animals in various ways, including adopting pets, providing pet-sitting services, volunteering, or walking dogs. Respondents shared their considerations when choosing an animal companion, such as balancing their lifestyle with the pet's needs, space, time, and financial resources. Some, due to limitations like living environment or busy schedules, cannot currently own pets but still want to connect with animals through volunteering or pet-sitting.

I also explored their feelings toward using an app for finding a pet or offering pet-related services. Trust and safety were key concerns, as people want reliable information about pet adoption or pet sitters. They emphasized the need for transparency in pet backgrounds, health, and behavioral information. The interviews revealed that users want features like trusted reviews, verified services, and simple, seamless tools to help them feel confident in their decisions, whether it's adopting a pet or seeking pet care assistance.

Prioritizing Pain Points

Trustworthy Information and Transparency: Across multiple user groups, the lack of reliable, detailed information is a critical pain point. Whether it's in finding adoption channels, understanding a pet's health, or discovering group-buying deals, users express significant frustration when they can't access transparent, trustworthy data. This pain point should be a top priority as it affects multiple segments of users.

Access to Animal Engagement Opportunities: For users without pets who want to volunteer or walk dogs, the absence of clear channels to access these opportunities is a key barrier. Addressing this pain point could open doors for them to build meaningful connections with animals and contribute to their care, making it a high priority.

Hand Sketches

What Was Done:

Based on interview results, I created initial hand sketches for the app. These sketches incorporated key features highlighted by users, such as clear navigation paths for pet-sitting, adoption, and detailed task information. The sketches were aimed at visualizing a user-friendly interface with a structured flow and straightforward options for connecting with pets.

What Was Learned:

The sketching process revealed important design priorities. Sketching also clarified how to improve navigation flow, ensuring a user-friendly experience that addresses user concerns about clarity, usability, and trust.

WireFrames

WireFrames

What Was Done:

Low-fidelity wireframes were created based on the initial hand sketches, laying out the foundational structure of the Pet Planet app. This wireframe focused on core elements such as navigation flow, feature placement, and task clarity, serving as a starting point for the high-fidelity prototype.

What Was Learned:

Building the wireframe helped solidify the layout and functionality needed to enhance usability. It also highlighted areas requiring refinement, such as ensuring intuitive navigation and clear task details, providing a strong foundation for developing a more polished, user-friendly prototype.

User Testing

Test Preparation

In this usability test for the Pet Planet app, my main goal was to assess how easily users could navigate key features, including pet-sitting and pet adoption options. I wanted to confirm that the app's interface made it simple for users to explore these services, gain confidence in the information provided, and understand the process of selecting pet-sitting tasks or finding a pet for adoption.

Running Usability Tests

I created simple scenarios for participants to use and test the existing features of my app, allowing them to engage with pet-sitting and adoption tasks in a real-world context. This approach helped reveal how users navigate and interact with key features in practical situations.

Usability Test Results

The usability test confirmed that Pet Planet has a strong foundation, with a clear, user-friendly interface and a design that resonates with pet lovers. To improve, the app should incorporate more detailed task and pet information, provide flexible adoption filters, and integrate more realistic visuals. Addressing these areas would make the app more functional and appealing, helping users feel confident about using it to find pet-sitting tasks or adopt a pet.

Conlusion

What Worked Well

  • Applealing Design: Cute, warm color scheme and clear layout contributed to a positive first impression.
  • Structured Flow: The step-by-step pet-sitting process and flowchart helped users navigate smoothly.
  • Pet-Sitting Experience: Users found the pet-sitting setup unique and well-organized, making it easy to explore opportunities.

Areas for Improvement

  • Task Details: Add pet type, payment info, location, and pet-specific characteristics to pet-sitting tasks.
  • Adoption Filters: Provide filters for breed, age, and location to streamline adoption searches.
  • UI Adjustments: Clarify back button function and merge date/time selectors for smoother navigation.

What's Next?

  • Add Pet Profiles:

    Include additional details for pets in both adoption and pet-sitting tasks (e.g., temperament, location, care needs).

  • Expand Filtering Options:

    Allow users to filter pets by specific criteria like breed, age, and health status.

  • Refine Navigation Elements:

    Improve back button functionality and simplify date/time selectors.

  • Introduce Additional Features:

    Add more functionalities, including find sitter, play date, volunteer, and buy pet options, to enhance user engagement and service variety.
